    4.

    A diagnostic test is developed to detect a disease present in 2.1% of the population. For a patient who has the disease, the test will accurately give a positive result 76% of the time. When the patient does not have the disease, the test will accurately give a negative result 98.2% of the time.
    ðŸ”—
    For a patient who receives a negative test, what is the probability they do not have the disease?
    ðŸ”—
    Solution.
    Let \(P\) be the event of receiving a positive test result and \(D\) be the event of having the disease. The given information is: \(\Pr(D) = 0.021, \Pr(P\mid D) = 0.76\text{,}\) and \(\Pr(P^c \mid D^c) = 0.982\text{.}\) Then, using Bayesâ€™ Theorem:
    \begin{align*} \Pr(D^c\mid P^c) \amp = \frac{\Pr(P^c \mid D^c)\Pr(D^c)}{\Pr(P^c \mid D^c)\Pr(D^c) + \Pr(P^c \mid D)\Pr(D)} \\ \amp = \frac{\Pr(P^c \mid D^c)(1 - \Pr(D))}{\Pr(P^c \mid D^c)(1 - \Pr(D)) + (1 - \Pr(P \mid D))\Pr(D)} \\ \amp = \frac{(0.982)(1 - 0.021)}{(0.982)(1 - 0.021) + (1 - 0.76)(0.021)} \\ \amp \approx \boxed{0.995} \end{align*}
    ðŸ”—
    ðŸ”—
