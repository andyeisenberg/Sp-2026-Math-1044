    3.

    A diagnostic test is developed to detect a disease present in 1.3% of the population. For a patient who has the disease, the test will accurately give a positive result 62% of the time. When the patient does not have the disease, the test will accurately give a negative result 99.4% of the time.
    ðŸ”—
    For a patient who receives a positive test, what is the probability they have the disease?
    ðŸ”—
    Solution.
    Let \(P\) be the event of receiving a positive test result and \(D\) be the event of having the disease. The given information is: \(\Pr(D) = 0.013, \Pr(P\mid D) = 0.62\text{,}\) and \(\Pr(P^c \mid D^c) = 0.994\text{.}\) Then, using Bayesâ€™ Theorem:
    \begin{align*} \Pr(D\mid P) = \frac{\Pr(P \mid D)\Pr(D)}{\Pr(P)} \amp = \frac{\Pr(P \mid D)\Pr(D)}{\Pr(P \mid D)\Pr(D) + \Pr(P \mid D^c)\Pr(D^c)} \\ \amp = \frac{\Pr(P \mid D)\Pr(D)}{\Pr(P \mid D)\Pr(D) + (1 - \Pr(P^c \mid D^c))(1 - \Pr(D))} \\ \amp = \frac{(0.62)(0.013)}{(0.62)(0.013) + (1 - 0.994)(1 - 0.013)} \\ \amp \approx \boxed{0.576} \end{align*}
